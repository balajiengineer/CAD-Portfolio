# 📌 Project Overview

This project was completed as part of a Mechanical Design Assessment for the conceptual development of a **Dual-Axis Solar Panel Gimbal Mechanism** intended for spacecraft solar array tracking.

The proposed mechanism provides independent pitch and yaw motion (±45°) for a 2 kg deployable solar panel while emphasizing lightweight construction, structural stiffness, manufacturability, modularity and high positioning accuracy.

The concept was developed following aerospace engineering practices including mechanism selection, actuator selection, material selection, preliminary engineering calculations, DFMA, GD&T philosophy and design validation planning.

# 🔄 Working Principle

The mechanism provides independent motion through two rotational axes.

### Pitch Axis

A BLDC servo motor coupled with a harmonic drive gearbox rotates the solar panel about the pitch axis while maintaining high positioning accuracy and minimal backlash.

### Yaw Axis

A second BLDC servo motor rotates the complete pitch assembly about the vertical axis, allowing independent yaw motion without affecting pitch orientation.
